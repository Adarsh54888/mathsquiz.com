Major Contributions:

Theory of Special Relativity (1905): Einstein introduced the theory of special relativity, fundamentally changing the understanding of space, time, and energy. Photoelectric Effect (1905): In a groundbreaking paper, Einstein explained the photoelectric effect, demonstrating that light could be thought of as discrete packets of energy called quanta (later termed photons). This work laid the foundation for quantum theory and earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.
Theory of General Relativity (1915): Einstein extended his theory of relativity to include gravity, proposing that massive objects cause a curvature in space-time, which we perceive as gravity. This theory was confirmed in 1919 during a solar eclipse, when light from stars was observed bending around the sun, as Einstein had predicted.
